Spatio-temporal maps of Plasmodium falciparum transmission around the perimeter of Majete Wildlife Reserve, Chikwawa District, Southern Malawi

Supplementary information for the paper "Identifying fine-scale spatiotemporal patterns in Plasmodium falciparum transmission by monitoring parasite prevalence and entomological inoculation rate" by Benjamin Amoah, Robert S. McCann, Alinune N. Kabaghe, Monicah Mburu, Michael G. Chipeta, Paula Moraga, Steven Gowelo, Tinashe Tizifa, Henk van den Berg, Themba Mzilahowa, Willem Takken, Michele vanVugt, Kamija S. This is an HTML Canvas flipbook that displays key frames of an animation in a more controllable manner than in a video player. If you are reading this text your browser does not support HTML Canvas.

Top panels: Prevalence mapping.

  • Left panel: median P. falciparum entomological inoculation rate (PfEIR).
  • Centre panel: mean P. falciparum parasite prevalence (PfPR) for children 0.5-5 y/o.
  • Right panel: mean PfPR in women 14-49 y/o.

Bottom panels: Exceedance probability mapping.

  • Left panel: probability that PfEIR exceeds 0.1 infective bites/person/month.
  • Centre panel: probability that PfPR in children 0.5-5 y/o exceeds 31%.
  • Right panel: probability that PfPR in women 14-49 y/o exceeds 17%.
  • Red areas demarcate "hotspots" where the exceedance probability is at least 0.9.
