    Alder Hey Hospital Prediction : Predicting emergency admissions in a children's hospital

    ---
title: Alder Hey Hospital Prediction
description: Predicting emergency admissions in a children's hospital
people:
 - rowlings
 - moraga
 - diggle
tags:
 - time_series
 - nhs
 - forecasting
map:  -2.898476278359631, 53.420175096345801
links:
 - url: http://www.alderhey.co.uk/
   description: Alder Hey Hospital
 - url: http://www.maths.lancs.ac.uk/~rowlings/Chicas/AlderHey/
   description: Demo site with simulated data
image:
 name: alderhey.png
 caption: Web site showing forecasted admissions with prediction intervals
---


    
Alder Hey Children's Hospital's existing method for forecasting
admissions was based on admission counts in previous years - a bit
like trying to predict tomorrow's weather based on the weather a year
ago. We know it will be roughly right, but won't recognise that we
have "hot spells" or "cold spells" of weather away from the norm that
last a few days. Similarly, but for unknown reasons, admission numbers
show fluctuations away from the simple forecast model the hospital was
using.

    


    
We constructed a statistical forecasting system that uses the
historical data to get the average behaviour and modulates its
predictions based on recent data, producing forecasts that are more
accurate and have an associated measure of precision.

    


    
The daily predictions are delivered via an interactive web site
accessible to hospital staff only. You can view
the demo site
which uses simulated data only.

---

    
Bluetongue is a viral disease of sheep and cattle that has spread across Europe into the UK
in recent years. The virus is spread by biting midges across animal populations.

    

    
This project, funded by the BBSRC, will investigate several aspects of the disease, including:

      

    • Computer simulation modelling of viral spread
      • 

    • Midge capture, with species and DNA typing across farms
      • 

    • Spatial analysis and mapping of midge capture data
      • 

    

    

    
The project is being led by staff from the Liverpool Vet School, with Chicas
working on the spatial analysis part of the project.

    Meningitis Forecasting : Watching out for epidemics in Africa

    ---
title: Meningitis Forecasting
description: Watching out for epidemics in Africa
people: 
  - taylor
  - diggle
  - agier
  - rowlings
tags:
  - spatial
  - africa
  - meningitis
image:
  name: meningitis.png
  caption: Typical monthly pattern of incidence across the belt (from WHO)
links:
  - url: http://www.meningvax.org/epidemic-updates.php
    description: WHO Meningitis Vaccine Project updates
map: 6.172669456464479, 15.267395668554396
---

    
Meningitis is a major health problem across a wide swath of Africa, the so-called "Meningitis Belt".
Within this belt, intense localised outbreaks of meningitis can occur. The current vaccination 
strategy is reactive, treating areas when prevalence hits a threshold. We aim to produce a 
predictive model that could direct vaccination schemes to areas of high epidemic probability.
Our method uses dynamic regression models in space and time to produce predictions of the
meningitis rate.

    

    
There is also concern that climate change is causing the belt to spread, affecting new
populations across Africa.
